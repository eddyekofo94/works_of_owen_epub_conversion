CHAPTER 25

OF THE COMMUNION OF SAINTS,

THE FIFTH PRIVILEGE OF BELIEVERS.

Q. 1. What is the communion of saints?

A. An holy conjunction120 between all God’s people, wrought by their participation of the same Spirit, whereby we are all made members of that one body whereof Christ is head. Song of Solomon 6:9; Jeremiah 32:39; John 17:22; 1 Corinthians 12:12; Ephesians 4:3-6, 13; 1 John 1:3, 6, 7.

Q. 2. Of what sort is this union?

A. First, spiritual and internal, in the enjoyment of the same Spirit and graces, — which is the union of the Hebrews church catholic; secondly, external and ecclesiastical, in the same outward ordinances, — which is the union of particular congregations. 1 Corinthians 12:12,13; Ephesians 2:16, 19-22; 1 Corinthians 10:17; John 17:11, 21, 22; John 10:16; 1:11. 1 Corinthians 1:10,11; Romans 12:5; 1 Corinthians 12:27,28; Ephesians 4:11-13; Philippians 5:2; Colossians 3:15; 1 Peter 3:8.
