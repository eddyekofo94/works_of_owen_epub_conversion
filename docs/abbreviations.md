# Table of Abbreviations

The following abbreviations are used throughout the footnotes and editorial notes to refer to standard critical editions, translations, and collections of patristic and scholastic works.

| Abbreviation | Full Title | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **ANF** | *Ante-Nicene Fathers* | The Ante-Nicene Fathers: The Writings of the Fathers Down to A.D. 325. Edited by Alexander Roberts and James Donaldson. |
| **NPNF1** | *Nicene and Post-Nicene Fathers, Series 1* | A Select Library of the Nicene and Post-Nicene Fathers of the Christian Church, First Series. Edited by Philip Schaff. (Primarily covers Augustine and Chrysostom). |
| **NPNF2** | *Nicene and Post-Nicene Fathers, Series 2* | A Select Library of the Nicene and Post-Nicene Fathers of the Christian Church, Second Series. Edited by Philip Schaff and Henry Wace. |
| **PG** | *Patrologia Graeca* | *Patrologiae Cursus Completus, Series Graeca*. Edited by J.-P. Migne. (Standard reference for Greek patristic texts). |
| **PL** | *Patrologia Latina* | *Patrologiae Cursus Completus, Series Latina*. Edited by J.-P. Migne. (Standard reference for Latin patristic texts). |
| **Battles** | *Institutes (Battles ed.)* | John Calvin, *Institutes of the Christian Religion*. Translated by Ford Lewis Battles, edited by John T. McNeill. (Library of Christian Classics). |
| **Aquin. ST** | *Summa Theologica* | Thomas Aquinas, *Summa Theologica*. (References usually provided by Part, Question, and Article, e.g., I, Q. 1, Art. 1). |
| **Turretin, Inst.** | *Institutes of Elenctic Theology* | Francis Turretin, *Institutes of Elenctic Theology*. (Translated by George Musgrave Giger, edited by James T. Dennison Jr.). |
| **Crell. de Natur. Spir. Sanc.** | *De Natura Spiritus Sancti* | Johannes Crellius (Johann Crell), *On the Nature of the Holy Spirit*. (17th-century Socinian text). |
| **Schlichting. de Trinitat.** | *De Trinitate* | Jonas Schlichting (Schlichtingius), *De Trinitate, de Moralibus Veteris et Novi Testamenti Praeceptis, Adversus Balthasarem Meisnerum*. |
| **Bellar. de Justif.** | *De Justificatione* | Robert Bellarmine, *De Justificatione* (On Justification), usually from his *Disputationes de Controversiis*. |
| **Maimon. More Nebuch.** | *Moreh Nevuchim* | Moses Maimonides, *The Guide for the Perplexed* (*Moreh Nevuchim*). |

## Usage Note
When a footnote provides a modern citation, it will typically provide the author, work, and the standard abbreviation in brackets. For example: `[PL 42.103]` refers to volume 42, column 103 of Migne's *Patrologia Latina*.
